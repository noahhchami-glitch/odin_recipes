Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

A creamy, comforting homemade Italian spaghetti carbonara

made with perfectly cooked pasta, crispy pancetta, and a rich sauce of eggs and Parmesan.

Quick to prepare yet full of authentic flavor, this classic dish is indulgent without being heavy.

Perfect for a cozy weeknight dinner.

Ingredients

  • 200-250g Spaghetti
  • 100 g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • large eggs + 1 egg yolk
  • 50 g freshly grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt for the pasta water

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti until al dente.

    Reserve about ½ cup of the pasta water, then drain.

  2. Cook the pancetta/guanciale: While the pasta cooks, heat a pan over medium heat

    and cook the diced pancetta until its crispy and golden. Turn off the heat.

  3. Mix the eggs and cheese: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolk, and grated cheese until smooth.

    Add a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper.

  4. Combine pasta and pancetta: Add the drained spaghetti to the pan with the pancetta

    (still warm, but not on direct heat) and toss to coat.

  5. Add the egg mixture: Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, tossing quickly to create a creamy sauce.

    Add a splash of reserved pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce.

    The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs gently—don’t scramble them!

  6. Serve immediately: Plate the pasta, sprinkle extra cheese and black pepper on top, and enjoy your authentic Italian carbonara.
