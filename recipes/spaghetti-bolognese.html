Spaghetti Bolognese

Description

A hearty, flavorful homemade Italian spaghetti Bolognese

made with tender minced meat, ripe tomatoes, and a rich blend of herbs and aromatics. Slow-simmered to perfection,

the sauce clings to each strand of pasta, creating a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ideal for a cozy dinner that tastes like it came straight from an Italian kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 200-250g Spaghetti
  • 250 g ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1–2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, finely diced
  • 1 celery stalk, finely diced
  • 400 g canned chopped tomatoes (or passata)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano or Italian herbs
  • 1–2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Freshly grated Parmesan, for serving

Steps

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Finely chop the onion, garlic, carrot, and celery.

  2. Cook the base: Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté the onion,

    carrot, celery, and garlic until soft and fragrant.

  3. Brown the meat: Add the ground beef (or beef/pork mix) and cook until browned,

    breaking up any lumps with a spoon.

  4. Add tomato flavors: Stir in the tomato paste, then add the canned chopped tomatoes. Mix well.

  5. Season and simmer: Add dried oregano or Italian herbs, salt, and pepper.

    Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer gently for 25–40 minutes, stirring occasionally.

    Add a splash of water or stock if it gets too thick.

  6. Cook the pasta: Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente.

    Drain and reserve a little pasta water.

  7. Combine and serve:Toss the cooked spaghetti with the Bolognese sauce, adding a little reserved pasta water if needed.

    Serve hot, topped with freshly grated Parmesan.
